ANSWER A:
**Recommendation**
`CheckpointRetentionPolicy` in Flink 2.2.0 has three choices:

- `RETAIN_ON_CANCELLATION`: keep checkpoints on both failure and cancellation; manual cleanup is required after cancel.
- `RETAIN_ON_FAILURE`: keep checkpoints on failure, but delete them on cancellation.
- `NEVER_RETAIN_AFTER_TERMINATION`: always clean up when the job reaches a terminal state.

The internal mapping is driven from externalized checkpoint cleanup settings: `DELETE_ON_CANCELLATION` maps to `RETAIN_ON_FAILURE`, `RETAIN_ON_CANCELLATION` maps to `RETAIN_ON_CANCELLATION`, and disabled externalized checkpoints map to `NEVER_RETAIN_AFTER_TERMINATION`.
